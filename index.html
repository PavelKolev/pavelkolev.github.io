Pavel Kolev




About

I obtained my doctoral degree on 2018 in the Computer Science Department at Saarland University, where I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Dr. Kurt Mehlhorn and Dr. Karl Bringmann. During my doctoral studies, I was also affiliated with the Algorithms and Complexity Department at Max Planck Institute for Informatics and the Cluster of Excellence on "Multimodal Computing and Interaction".

Afterwards, I spent a year as a Postdoctoral researcher at the Algorithms and Complexity Department at Max Planck Institute for Informatics, hosted by Prof. Dr. Kurt Mehlhorn.




Email: pavelkolevbggmail.com

Links: LinkedIn | GitHub | DBLP | Google Scholar

Research Interests

    I am interested in Machine Learning. In particular, the areas of reinforcement learning, multi-armed bandit, and attention mechanisms (aka Transformers, Performers). In addition, I have experience in the areas of robust low rank approximation, spectral clustering, linear and convex optimization, and dynamical systems.
